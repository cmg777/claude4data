## Key Findings (Consistent Across Both Implementations)

From both Python and R notebooks:

1. **Textbook Solow overstates capital share:**
   - Implied α ≈ 0.60 (vs. theoretical 1/3)
   - Missing variable bias from omitting human capital

2. **Augmented Solow model performs better:**
   - R² improves from 0.59 to 0.78
   - Implied α ≈ 1/3 (physical capital)
   - Implied β ≈ 1/3 (human capital)
   - Both factors equally important

3. **Strong conditional convergence:**
   - Poor countries grow faster given their fundamentals
   - Convergence speed: 1.4-2.1% per year
   - Half-life: 34-49 years

4. **Model explains ~80% of income variation**
   - Differences in physical capital
   - Differences in human capital
   - Differences in population growth
