Introduction

Regional economic disparities remain a central concern in development economics. Understanding the relationship between GDP per capita and life expectancy across regions is critical for designing effective policy interventions.

This paper uses a novel synthetic dataset to examine these patterns, employing both parametric and non-parametric methods (Keola, Andersson, and Hall 2015).

Our results suggest a strong positive correlation between economic output and health outcomes, with significant heterogeneity across geographic clusters.

This study contributes to the literature in two ways. First, we provide a multi-language reproducible workflow. Second, we demonstrate how modern tools can enhance transparency in empirical research.

The rest of this article is organized as follows. Section 2 provides an overview of the related literature. Section 3 introduces the data and methods. Section 4 presents the results and discussion. Finally, Section 5 offers some concluding remarks.

Related literature

The relationship between income and health has been extensively studied since the seminal work of Keola, Andersson, and Hall (2015). Recent advances in spatial econometrics have enabled more nuanced analyses of regional disparities1.
